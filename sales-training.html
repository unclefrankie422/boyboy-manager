一、培训背景与目标

本次培训针对一位从公司内部转岗到销售岗位的女同事。她在公司已经工作了一段时间，对公司的产品、内部流程和企业文化都有较好的了解，这是她转岗的最大优势——她不需要从零开始熟悉公司和产品。但她之前没有做过销售工作，所以本次培训的重点不是教她认识产品，而是教她如何把产品卖出去，如何跟客户建立关系，如何成为一名合格的销售人员。

培训周期为三个月，分为三个阶段：第一个月打基础，第二个月跟实战，第三个月独立跑。除了由我负责系统的职业培训之外，华东和华南两位同事也会参与进来，把他们的销售经验分享给她——华东同事定期过来带她跑客户，华南同事在日常工作中给她提供陪伴和技术支持。三个月后的目标是：她能够独立拜访和维护C档客户，在B档客户面前能够配合资深同事完成拜访，对公司的销售流程和客户管理有完整的理解和实践能力。

二、她的优势与需要补足的地方

在做培训计划之前，我们首先要客观地认识这位新同事的优势和短板。她在公司内部工作了一段时间，这给她带来了几个天然的优势。第一，她熟悉产品。公司的产品线、技术参数、应用场景，她比外面新招来的人要了解得多，这意味着产品培训这一块可以大大压缩时间。第二，她了解公司流程。从下单到出货、从报价到收款，这些内部操作她可能比销售人员还清楚，这对将来服务客户是很大的加分项。第三，她对公司有归属感，稳定性高，这一点在当前公司急需稳定团队的情况下非常重要。

但她需要补足的地方也很明显。首先，她没有销售经验，不知道如何开口跟客户建立第一次联系，不知道如何引导客户的需求，不知道如何处理客户的异议和拒绝。其次，她不熟悉市场，不知道客户在哪里、竞争对手是谁、这个行业是怎么做生意的。第三，她需要建立自信，从内勤角色转到外勤角色，从被动等待变成主动出击，这个心理上的转变需要时间和引导。

总的来说，她的起点比外面招来的人高，因为产品关和流程关她已经过了。我们需要集中精力帮她过的，是销售关、市场关和心理关。

三、培训分工

本次培训由我协助总经理统筹执行，华东和华南同事配合支持，三方各有侧重，协同推进。

我（顾问）——协助总经理执行培训

我的核心职责是协助总经理规划和落地整个培训方案。具体包括四个方面：

销售心理学——教她读懂客户的潜台词，建立信任，处理拒绝，理解客户的决策心理；

沟通技巧——怎么提问才能挖出真实需求、怎么倾听才能让客户愿意说、怎么回应才能推动成交；

职业素养——时间管理、客户管理、自我管理，帮助她完成从"执行者"到"主动者"的角色转变；

心态建设——被拒绝后怎么调整、业绩压力怎么应对、遇到瓶颈怎么突破。

培训形式以每周一次的一对一谈话和实战案例讨论为主，同时我负责协助总经理统筹整体培训节奏，确保三个月按计划推进、各阶段目标达成。

华东同事——定期过来协助

华东同事销售经验丰富，会定期过来协助。主要做两件事：一是带她出去跑客户，让她亲眼看到有经验的销售怎么开场、怎么介绍产品、怎么处理异议、怎么促成成交；二是跑完之后跟她复盘，聊聊刚才的过程——为什么这样讲、为什么那样回应，把经验自然地分享出来。华东同事不需要常驻，定期过来带她跑几天，核心是让她看到真实销售的样子。

华南同事——日常陪伴协助

华南同事日常在华南，可以随时提供支持。他的协助主要体现在：每天跟她交流学习进展和遇到的问题；在技术层面给予支撑——当客户提出深入的技术问题时协助应对；带她一起跑C档客户，从陪同观察到逐步放手让她独立；帮她熟悉华南地区的客户情况和市场环境。华南同事的优势是技术扎实、日常在场，有他在，新同事有一个可靠的依靠。

四、三个月培训计划

第一个月：打基础——从"知道"到"会做"

第一个月的重点是观察和模仿。她不需要独立面对客户，而是跟在华东同事和华南同事身边，看他们怎么做、听他们怎么说。华东同事定期过来，每次带她跑几天客户，让她密集地观察成熟销售的全流程。跑完之后跟她复盘：刚才客户为什么那样说？什么时候开始对产品感兴趣的？如果让你来，你会怎么讲？这种聊天式的经验分享比正式培训更自然，也更容易吸收。

华南同事日常带她做基础工作：一起整理客户资料，教她看客户档案、记录拜访信息；带她拜访C档客户，让她从旁观察，逐步尝试做简单的自我介绍和产品介绍。我每周跟她做一次一对一谈话，帮她建立销售思维框架——客户买的不是产品，是产品能解决的问题；教她怎么提问——不是"您需要什么"，而是"您目前用什么？有什么不满意的地方？"；帮她建立自信——看到已有的优势，也正视需要学习的地方。

第一个月检验标准：能完整复述一次销售拜访的全过程，能说出客户的需求点和痛点，能独立完成一次基本的产品介绍。

第二个月：跟实战——从"会看"到"能说"

第二个月的重点是参与和尝试。她开始真正进入销售场景。华东同事过来时，拜访客户的流程变成"半接手"模式——华东同事负责开场和建立关系，她负责产品介绍和报价说明。有老同事在旁边兜底，她在真实场景中练习，不会出大问题。

华南同事这边开始放手让她独立拜访C档客户——不是一个人去，而是华南同事在旁边或外面等，沟通全程由她主导。拜访结束后给她反馈：哪里讲得好、哪里可以改进、客户哪个表情说明在犹豫。这种"你做我看、做完聊"的方式是销售培训最有效的方法。

我每周跟她做一次实战案例讨论，让她带一个当周遇到的实际案例来——成功的或失败的都可以。一起拆解：客户为什么那样说？回应是否得当？换一种方式会怎样？通过反复拆解，建立销售的判断力和应变能力。同时教她客户管理的基本方法：客户分级、拜访计划、记录跟进。

第二个月检验标准：能独立完成C档客户的拜访和跟进，在B档客户拜访中能承担至少一半的沟通，客户反馈正面。

第三个月：独立跑——从"能说"到"能成交"

第三个月的重点是独立和成果。C档客户完全由她自己负责——从拜访、报价、跟进到成交，��流程独立走完。华南同事不再陪同，但每天会聊聊当天的拜访情况。B档客户方面，角色反过来——她主导沟通，华南同事在旁边补充和兜底。

华东同事定期过来，主要做两件事：一是看看她独立拜访的状态，给一些更高阶的建议；二是带她去见一两个A档客户，让她见识大客户是怎么维护的——不是为了让她接手A档，而是让她看到标杆在哪里。

我这个月的重点是帮她做总结和提升。三个月培训即将结束，我们一起回顾：学到了什么、最大的进步在哪里、还有哪些不足、下一步怎么走。同时帮她建立一套属于自己的销售方法论——不是照搬别人的，而是结合她自己的性格和优势，形成她独特的销售风格。

第三个月检验标准：C档客户独立管理、零投诉；B档客户能配合完成拜访并得到客户认可；能独立制定周拜访计划和客户跟进计划；对自己的销售能力有清晰的自我认知。

六、考核标准

培训不是走形式，三个月后我们要有明确的考核标准来判断她是否合格。

考核不是为了卡人，而是为了帮她看清自己的成长和不足。如果某个方面不达标，不是淘汰她，而是针对性地加强培训。

七、几点提醒

这个培训方案能否落地，取决于三方各司其职：

华东同事——定期过来，把经验分享出来。带她跑客户之后多聊聊，让她理解"为什么这样做"，而不只是"看到了什么"。

华南同事——日常陪伴，逐步放手。不要一直帮她做，而是刻意推动她从"跟着看"到"自己试"再到"独立做"。技术能力强是你的优势，把这份能力传递给她。

我（协助总经理）——帮她建立销售思维框架。技术可以在工作中积累，但面对客户的心态、处理压力的能力、销售的方法论，需要有人专门引导。我每周一次的一对一谈话和案例讨论，是这次培训的核心引擎。

培训成功的标准不是三个月后能做多少业绩，而是她能否独立面对客户、知道自己该做什么、遇到问题知道怎么求助。业绩是水到渠成的事，能力才是培训的目标。

1. Training Background and Objectives

This training program is designed for a female colleague who is transitioning from an internal role to a sales position. She has been working at the company for some time and already has a good understanding of our products, internal processes, and company culture. This is her greatest advantage — she does not need to start from zero in learning about the company and its products. However, she has no prior sales experience. Therefore, the focus of this training is not on teaching her about our products, but on teaching her how to sell them — how to build relationships with clients, and how to become a competent salesperson.

The training period is three months, divided into three phases: Month 1 — building the foundation; Month 2 — hands-on practice; Month 3 — independent operation. In addition to the structured professional training that I will provide, our East China and South China colleagues will also participate by sharing their sales experience — the East China colleague will visit regularly to take her on client visits, while the South China colleague will provide daily support and technical assistance. The goal after three months: she should be able to independently visit and manage Tier C clients, support Tier B client visits alongside experienced colleagues, and have a complete understanding and practical capability in the company's sales processes and client management.

2. Her Strengths and Areas for Development

Before designing the training plan, we need to objectively assess her strengths and gaps. Having worked internally at the company for some time gives her several natural advantages. First, she knows the products. She understands our product lines, technical specifications, and application scenarios far better than any external hire would — which means we can significantly compress the time spent on product training. Second, she understands company processes. From order placement to delivery, from quotation to payment collection — she may know these internal operations better than some salespeople, and this is a major asset when serving clients. Third, she has a sense of belonging and stability, which is especially valuable given the company's current need for a steady team.

However, the areas she needs to develop are also clear. First, she has no sales experience. She does not know how to initiate that first conversation with a client, how to uncover client needs, or how to handle objections and rejection. Second, she is unfamiliar with the market. She does not know where the clients are, who the competitors are, or how business is done in this industry. Third, she needs to build confidence — transitioning from a back-office role to a front-line role, from passive waiting to proactive outreach. This psychological shift takes time and guidance.

In summary, her starting point is higher than an external hire because she has already cleared the product and process hurdles. What we need to focus on is helping her clear the sales, market, and mindset hurdles.

3. Training Roles

This training is coordinated by me, assisting the General Manager, with support from our East China and South China colleagues. Each role has a distinct focus.

Me (Consultant) — Assisting General Manager in Executing Training

My core responsibility is to assist the General Manager in planning and executing the entire training program. This covers four areas:

Sales Psychology — teaching her to read clients' subtext, build trust, handle rejection, and understand client decision-making psychology;

Communication Skills — how to ask questions that uncover real needs, how to listen so clients open up, how to respond in ways that move toward closing;

Professional Discipline — time management, client management, self-management, helping her transition from "executor" to "proactive initiator";

Mindset Development — how to bounce back after rejection, how to handle performance pressure, how to break through bottlenecks.

Training takes the form of weekly one-on-one conversations and real-case discussions. I also assist the General Manager in overseeing the overall training pace to ensure the three-month plan stays on track and each phase's goals are met.

East China Colleague — Regular Visits to Assist

The East China colleague has considerable sales experience and will visit regularly to assist. His main role is twofold: first, take her on client visits so she can observe firsthand how an experienced salesperson opens, presents products, handles objections, and closes deals; second, debrief with her afterward — discussing why things were said a certain way and why certain responses were chosen — sharing his experience in a natural, conversational way. He does not need to be based here permanently. Regular visits of a few days each are sufficient, with the core objective of letting her see what real selling looks like.

South China Colleague — Daily On-Site Assistance

The South China colleague is on-site daily and can provide ongoing support. His assistance includes: daily conversations about her learning progress and challenges; technical support — when clients raise in-depth technical questions, he helps her respond; accompanying her on Tier C client visits, gradually stepping back as she gains confidence; helping her become familiar with the South China client base and market environment. His strengths are his solid technical skills and daily presence, giving the new colleague a reliable partner to lean on.

4. Three-Month Training Plan

Month 1: Building the Foundation — From "Knowing" to "Doing"

The focus of Month 1 is observation and imitation. She does not need to face clients independently. Instead, she shadows the East China and South China colleagues — watching how they work, listening to how they speak. The East China colleague visits regularly, each time taking her on a few days of client visits for intensive exposure to a seasoned salesperson's full process. After each round, he debriefs with her: Why did the client say that? When did you notice them showing interest? If you had to do it again, how would you approach it? This conversational style of experience sharing is more natural and easier to absorb than formal training.

The South China colleague works with her daily on foundational tasks: organizing client data together, teaching her to read client files and record visit information; taking her to visit Tier C clients, letting her observe from the side, then gradually trying simple self-introductions and product presentations. I have a weekly one-on-one session with her covering: building a sales mindset — clients don't buy because your product is good, but because it solves their problem; teaching her to ask the right questions — not "What do you need?" but "What are you currently using? What problems have you had?"; building confidence — helping her recognize her existing strengths while honestly addressing what she needs to learn.

Month 1 checkpoint: Can fully recount a complete sales visit process, identify client needs and pain points, and independently deliver a basic product presentation.

Month 2: Hands-on Practice — From "Watching" to "Speaking"

The focus of Month 2 is participation and experimentation. She begins to truly enter sales scenarios. When the East China colleague visits, the client visit format shifts to "half-handover" — the East China colleague handles the opening and relationship-building, while she takes over the product presentation and pricing discussion. With an experienced colleague as backup, she practices in real scenarios without major risk.

On the South China side, she starts independently visiting Tier C clients — not alone, but with the South China colleague nearby or waiting outside, while she leads the entire conversation. After each visit, he gives her feedback: what went well, what could improve, which client expression signaled hesitation. This "you do it, I watch, then we talk" model is the most effective way to train salespeople.

My weekly focus is real-case discussions. She brings an actual case from that week — success or failure. Together we analyze: Why did the client say that? Was your response appropriate? What if you had responded differently? Through repeated case analysis, she develops sales judgment and adaptability. I also teach her basic client management: client tiering, visit planning, recording and follow-up.

Month 2 checkpoint: Can independently visit and follow up with Tier C clients; can handle at least half of the communication during Tier B client visits; client feedback is positive.

Month 3: Independent Operation — From "Speaking" to "Closing"

The focus of Month 3 is independence and results. Tier C clients are fully under her management — from visits, quotations, follow-ups, to closing deals, the entire process is handled independently. The South China colleague no longer accompanies her but chats daily about how visits went. For Tier B clients, the roles reverse — she leads the communication, while the South China colleague provides supplementary support.

The East China colleague visits regularly to do two things: observe her independent visit state and provide higher-level suggestions; and take her to see one or two Tier A clients, so she can witness how major accounts are maintained — not to take over Tier A, but to see where the benchmark lies.

My focus this month is helping her summarize and elevate. As the three months draw to a close, we review together: what she has learned, her greatest progress, remaining gaps, and next steps. I also help her build her own sales methodology — not a copy of someone else's, but one shaped by her personality and strengths into her unique style.

Month 3 checkpoint: Tier C clients independently managed with zero complaints; can collaborate on Tier B visits with client recognition; can independently create weekly visit plans and follow-up plans; has a clear self-assessment of her sales capabilities.

7. A Few Reminders

Whether this training plan succeeds depends on each of us fulfilling our role:

East China Colleague — Visit regularly and share experience. After taking her on client visits, spend time debriefing so she understands "why things are done this way," not just "what happened."

South China Colleague — Daily support with gradual release. Don't keep doing things for her — deliberately push her from "watch me" to "try it yourself" to "do it independently." Your strong technical skills are an advantage — pass that capability on to her.

Me (Assisting GM) — Help her build a sales thinking framework. Techniques can be picked up on the job, but the sales mindset, client-facing attitude, and pressure-handling ability need dedicated guidance. My weekly one-on-one sessions and case discussions are the core engine of this training.

The measure of successful training is not how much revenue she generates after three months. It is whether she can independently face clients, know what she needs to do, and know how to seek help when facing difficulties. Revenue will come naturally. Capability is the goal.
